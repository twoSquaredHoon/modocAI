HOOK:
Is your 7-month-old baby's fever returning even after an ear infection?

BODY:
It's scary when a fever keeps coming back. Many parents wonder if they should wipe their baby with lukewarm water. But current guidelines say to avoid lukewarm sponge baths. They make your 7-month-old uncomfortable and aren't very effective. Instead, dress your baby lightly. Keep the room at 75 to 79 degrees Fahrenheit. Offer breast milk or formula often. You can give acetaminophen every four hours. Watch for these serious signs: fever lasting over three days. Breathing that is rough, heavy, or rapid. Feeding less than half of normal. Or no wet diapers for over eight hours.

RELIEF:
If breathing is rough, feeding drops, or there are no wet diapers for over eight hours, revisit the clinic immediately. If the fever continues tomorrow, see your pediatrician.
