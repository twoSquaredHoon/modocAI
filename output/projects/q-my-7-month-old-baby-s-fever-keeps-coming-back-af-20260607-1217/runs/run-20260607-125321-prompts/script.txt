Is your 7-month-old's fever back? Don't use this common trick that makes them uncomfortable.

BODY:
Your 7-month-old has a recurring fever. Avoid lukewarm sponge baths. They're uncomfortable and ineffective. Instead, dress them lightly. Keep the room 75 to 79 degrees Fahrenheit. Offer breast milk or formula often. Give an appropriate acetaminophen dose four hours after the last. Watch for serious signs. If fever lasts over three days. If breathing gets rough or rapid. If feeding drops by half. Or no wet diapers for over eight hours.

RELIEF:
If breathing is rough, feeding drops, or no wet diapers for eight hours – see your doctor immediately. If the fever continues tomorrow, revisit your pediatrician.
